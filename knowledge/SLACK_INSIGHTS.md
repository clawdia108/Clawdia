## 1. PRODUCT INSIGHTS

### Core Product: Echo Pulse
- **What it is:** Short, recurring anonymous employee feedback surveys ("pulses") sent monthly via email or Slack. 5-6 questions, takes 2-3 minutes. Results include AI-generated summaries, team breakdowns, trend lines, and actionable checklists for managers.
- **Key features valued by customers:**
  - Anonymity without login ("anonymita bez prihlasovani")
  - Short questionnaire (5-6 questions, psychologically simple)
  - Automatic AI summarization of open-ended responses
  - Team/department breakdowns
  - Time trend visualization
  - Manager checklists and recommendations
  - AI chatbot for explaining results
  - Ability to run pulse on specific team only
- **Product direction (March 2026):**
  - New pulse topics being developed -- shifting from wellbeing-focused to more operational/execution themes ("tvrdsi, operational, min emoci, prakticka, uchopitelnejsi")
  - Multiple choice questions now map to specific organizational areas (leadership, communication, roles, etc.) for sharper diagnostics
  - CEO Dashboard concept ("cockpit pro CEO") combining analytics + AI-generated report + AI assistant for action planning
  - Self-signup "quick pulse" trial flow live at app.behavera.com/echo-pulse/try
  - echopulse.cz website launched on Vercel
  - UK market expansion underway

### Secondary Product: Assessments
- Used for hiring and leadership development
- Customers: Dworkin, Cocuma, Prazske sluzby, Hyundai (interested)
- Hyundai feedback: "liked simplicity and clarity" but won't use for hiring, considering for development

### What Customers Replace When They Buy Behavera
- Google Forms (manual NPS -- Grizly)

## 3. COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE

### Direct Competitors Mentioned
| Competitor | Key Weaknesses (from Slack) |
|---|---|
| **LutherOne** | Complex implementation, hard to operate. Retention problem: leaders stop using it, people get identified by writing style and punished, trust lost. "Slo implementace i obsluhy" |
| **Arnold/LMC** | "Impersonal, technical, long" -- customer feedback from Grizly who tried and abandoned it |
| **HiBob** | Too manual for HR. Good HRIS but survey features are basic |
| **Culture Amp** | Enterprise-focused, crushing smaller players with complexity and integrations |
| **Lattice** | Same as Culture Amp -- winning via completeness |
| **Google Forms** | Manual reading of open responses, no automatic summarization, no trends |
| **Engagement Multiplier** | UK-Chicago competitor targeting firms under 250 people. "Average starting score 68.9" |
| **TINYpulse** | Acquired by Limeade in 2021 for only $9.1M at 1.4x revenue. Classic "feature vs platform" problem |

### Behavera's Differentiation Points (from customer conversations)
1. **Simplicity** -- repeatedly the #1 thing mentioned. "Perfect for a leader who doesn't have time"
2. **Anonymity** -- MASSIVE difference vs 1:1s. "Only place they learn the truth"
3. **Speed** -- few minutes to fill, instant results
4. **AI summaries** -- auto-interpret open-ended responses
5. **Actionable outputs** -- not just data, but "what to do about it"
6. **Professional consultation** -- human layer on top of AI
7. **No login required** -- frictionless for respondents

### TINYpulse Case Study (competitive intel from Jana's deep research)
- Started with 1 question per week in 2012
- Hit $6.5M revenue by 2020 with 500+ customers
- Sold for $9.1M (1.4x revenue -- extremely low for SaaS)
- **Why they failed:** Too simple, firms "outgrew" them. 200 copycats appeared. 90 employees for revenue that 30 could handle.
- **Lesson for Behavera:** Don't be just a "feature" -- be the platform. But also don't over-complexify.
