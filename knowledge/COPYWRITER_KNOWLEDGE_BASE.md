## 4. TARGET PERSONAS & BUYING PSYCHOLOGY

### Persona 1: CEO (50-500+ employees)
- **Pain:** Doesn't know real cost of turnover. Sees it as "HR problem."
- **Trigger:** When they calculate that 15% turnover = 15M CZK/year
- **Language:** Business language, ROI, P&L impact, competitive advantage
- **Objection:** "We don't have time for this" → Answer: "It takes 5 minutes per person per quarter"
- **Decision style:** Wants proof, data, case studies. Reads on mobile at 6 AM with coffee
- **Hook style:** Shocking numbers, counterintuitive claims, short case stories

### Persona 2: HR Director / HR Manager
- **Pain:** Drowning in manual surveys, no tools, questioned about ROI
- **Trigger:** Survey fatigue, low response rates, can't prove impact to leadership
- **Language:** HR terminology OK but avoid academic jargon. Practical, "how-to" focused
- **Objection:** "We already do surveys" → Answer: "How much time does it take? What actions come from results?"
- **Decision style:** Risk-averse, needs to justify budget. Values peer proof (other HR leaders using it)
- **Hook style:** Comparison tables, step-by-step guides, templates

### Persona 3: Team Leader / Middle Manager
- **Pain:** Team morale issues, no feedback tools, caught between leadership and team
- **Trigger:** Key person threatening to leave, or post-survey "now what?"
- **Language:** Casual, practical. "What do I do Monday morning?"
- **Objection:** "My team is fine" → Answer: "Spoléhat na pocity nestačí. Data reveal what daily observation misses."
- **Decision style:** Needs buy-in from above. Champion who pushes internally
- **Hook style:** Relatable stories, quick wins, practical frameworks

### CEO Fears (from internal research)
- Fear of what they'll find out
- Fear that employees won't be honest
- Fear of creating expectations they can't meet
- Fear of wasting money on "soft" metrics
- "We'll measure when things calm down" (= never)

## 5. COPYWRITING VOICE & STYLE GUIDE

### Brand Voice (from blog-rewrite-prompt.md)
**"Jsi chytrý kolega, který sedí v kavárně a říká ti věci, které ti změní pohled."**

- Confident but not arrogant
- Smart but accessible
- Czech but modern
- Direct, punchy, short sentences
- Rhetorical questions, incomplete thoughts, direct speech — all OK
- Write as if CEO reads on mobile at 6 AM with coffee — grab them in 3 sentences

### NEVER Use These Words/Phrases
❌ "V dnešním dynamickém prostředí"
❌ "Synergické efekty"
❌ "Holistický přístup"
❌ "Komplexní řešení"
❌ "Paradigma"
❌ "Stakeholder" (unless truly needed)
❌ "Implementace" (unless technical context)
❌ "V dnešní době..."
❌ "Je důležité si uvědomit..."
❌ "Pojďme se podívat na..."

### DO Use
✅ Short sentences. Even fragments.
✅ Numbers that shock ("15 milionů. A to je konzervativní odhad.")
✅ Real customer quotes (only approved ones — see Case Studies section)
✅ Rhetorical questions ("Co se stane, když to ignorujete?")
✅ Section headings as cliffhangers
✅ LinkedIn-copy-paste-worthy sentences (marked `{/* linkedin-hook */}`)
✅ Czech + English terms together: "tiché odcházení (quiet quitting)"

### Content Rules
- Every article must be VALUABLE without the product — reader takes away insight, framework, or "aha moment"
- Reader should think: "Tohle bych dal kolegovi/šéfovi/HR ředitelce"
- CTA is natural conclusion, not aggressive sell
- Max 2 soft CTAs per article (one mid-text, one at end)
- FAQ section at end (3-5 questions) for Featured Snippets and AI discoverability
- 1200-1800 words per article (6-9 min read)
- Paragraphs max 3-4 sentences — airy text
- Tables, bullets, numbered lists for skimmability

### SEO Standards (2026)
- Title: max 60 chars, primary keyword at start, emotional/clickable
- Meta description: max 155 chars, keyword + value prop + micro CTA
- Natural keywords in H2/H3 — no stuffing
- Schema-friendly: definitions in H3 + paragraph, comparison tables, ordered lists for processes
- Czech + English keyword variants for AI search reach
- Internal linking: 2-3 related articles per post
- Blockquotes for key insights (AI models and Featured Snippets prefer clear, quotable statements)

## 6. SALES PSYCHOLOGY FRAMEWORKS (from ebook library)

### From "The Psychology of Selling" (Brian Tracy)
- Selling is a transfer of enthusiasm
- People buy emotionally and justify logically
- The #1 skill: asking great questions and listening
- Reciprocity: give value first, the sale follows naturally
- People buy from those they trust — trust = competence + likability

### From "$100M Offers" (Alex Hormozi)
- Make offers so good people feel stupid saying no
- Value equation: Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood / Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
- Stack value, don't discount price
- Remove risk: "Try it free on one team" = Behavera's approach

### From "Predictable Revenue" (Aaron Ross)
- Specialize sales roles (SDR vs AE vs CS)
- Cold outreach works when personalized + relevant
- "Nailing a Niche" — dominate one segment before expanding
- Assembly line approach: leads → qualified → closed

### From "Sell More with Science" (David Hoffeld)
- Buyer's brain makes decisions based on 6 stimuli: self-centered, contrast, tangible, beginning/end, visual, emotional
- Framing effects: how you present options matters more than the options themselves
- Social proof is the most powerful persuasion tool in B2B
- Commitment & consistency: small "yes" leads to big "yes"

### From "Way of the Wolf" (Jordan Belfort) — Straight Line Selling
- Control the sale by staying on the "straight line" from opening to close
- Three Tens: prospect must rate you 10 on Product (certainty), You (trust), Company (trust)
- Tonality and body language > words
- Lower the action threshold + raise the pain threshold

### From "Cold Email Manifesto" (Alex Berman)
- Subject line = 80% of the email's success
- Personalization = showing you did homework, not mail-merge tricks
- Short emails (3-5 sentences max)
- One clear CTA per email
- Follow-up is where deals happen (5-7 touches minimum)

### From "People Buy You" (Jeb Blount)
- People do business with people they like and trust
- EQ > IQ in selling
- Likability + competence + trustworthiness = the trifecta
- Active listening is the most underrated sales skill

### From "The AI Edge" (Jeb Blount & Iannarino)
- AI saves time on research, personalization, follow-ups
- Human connection still closes deals — AI handles the logistics
- Use AI for: lead scoring, email drafts, meeting prep, call coaching
- Don't let AI replace the relationship

### SPIN Selling Framework (used in EchoDialer coaching)
- **S**ituation: Understand current state
- **P**roblem: Identify pain points
- **I**mplication: Show consequences of inaction
- **N**eed-payoff: Connect solution to their stated needs

## 7. HR & EMPLOYEE ENGAGEMENT SCIENCE (from ebook library)

### Core Engagement Model (from multiple sources)
Employee engagement is NOT satisfaction. Key distinction:
- **Satisfaction** = "I'm okay here" (passive)
- **Engagement** = "I care about this company's success and I'm willing to give extra" (active)

**Gallup's 3 Categories:**
1. Engaged (20-23% globally)
2. Not Engaged (59% — "quiet quitting")
3. Actively Disengaged (18% — toxic, undermining)

### The 12 Factors That Drive Engagement (synthesis from multiple books)
1. **Clear expectations** — Do I know what's expected of me?
2. **Resources & materials** — Do I have what I need to do my work?
3. **Opportunity to do best work** — Am I in the right role?
4. **Recognition** — Has someone praised my work in the last 7 days?
5. **Someone cares about me** — Does my manager/colleague care about me as a person?
6. **Development** — Is someone encouraging my growth?
7. **Opinions count** — Do my opinions seem to matter?
8. **Mission connection** — Does my company's mission make me feel important?
9. **Quality commitment** — Are my co-workers committed to quality?
10. **Best friend at work** — Do I have a close friend at work?
11. **Progress** — Has someone talked to me about my progress in last 6 months?
12. **Learning** — Have I had opportunities to learn and grow?

### Manager Impact (from "The Manager Factor" research)
- **Managers account for 70% of variance in team engagement** (Gallup)
- Bad managers don't just lose people — they lose the best people first
- Manager training has highest ROI of any engagement intervention
- "Middle management is the bottleneck" — common Czech company problem

### The Cost of Disengagement (key stats for copywriting)
- Disengaged employees cost 18% of their annual salary in lost productivity (Gallup)
- Teams with high engagement: +18% productivity, +23% profitability (Gallup meta-analysis)
- Replacement cost: 50-200% of annual salary depending on seniority (SHRM)
- Czech unemployment among lowest in EU → harder to replace → higher stakes
- 66% of people would quit if they felt unappreciated (Forbes study)

### Survey Science (from "Employee Surveys That Work" + "How to Make Sense of Survey Results")
- Annual surveys are too infrequent — quarterly + pulse checks ideal
- Survey fatigue is real — keep it short, show results, take action
- Response rate drivers: trust, brevity, visible follow-through
- The #1 killer of survey programs: asking but not acting
- Chatbot format increases engagement vs traditional form-based surveys

### Burnout Framework
- Burnout = chronic workplace stress not successfully managed (WHO)
- 3 dimensions: exhaustion, cynicism, reduced professional efficacy
- Prevention > cure: regular pulse checks catch early signals
- Remote work amplified burnout (always-on culture)
- "Quiet quitting" is a burnout defense mechanism

### Employee Well-being Model
- Well-being = not just absence of illness, but thriving
- 5 elements (Gallup): Career, Social, Financial, Physical, Community
- Company role: create conditions where work positively impacts well-being
- Not about wellness perks (fruit, yoga) — about structural factors: autonomy, purpose, fair workload, feedback, growth

### From "State of the Global Workplace 2025" (Gallup)
- Global engagement: ~23%
- Europe consistently lower than global average
- Manager quality = #1 factor in engagement across all regions
- AI disruption creating new anxiety layer → need for proactive engagement measurement

### From "Data-Driven HR" (Bernard Marr)
- People analytics is shifting from descriptive to predictive
- AI can identify flight risk before the employee knows they want to leave
- Ethical considerations: transparency, consent, avoiding "creepy analytics"
- The future: continuous listening, not periodic surveys

## 8. COGNITIVE BIASES & PERSUASION TOOLKIT

### From "The Cognitive Biases Compendium" (Murat Durmus) — 150+ biases
Key biases for copywriting:

| Bias | What It Is | How to Use in Copy |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Loss Aversion** | Losses feel 2x stronger than gains | "You're losing 15M CZK/year to silent turnover" |
| **Social Proof** | We follow what others do | Case studies, customer logos, "300+ Czech companies" |
| **Authority** | We trust experts | "According to Gallup...", scientific team credentials |
| **Anchoring** | First number frames all following | Start with high cost, then show savings |
| **Status Quo Bias** | Prefer current state | "What's the cost of doing nothing?" |
| **IKEA Effect** | We value what we help create | "Co-design your engagement program" |
| **Bandwagon** | Follow the crowd | "Leading Czech companies already use..." |
| **Scarcity** | Rare = valuable | Limited free pilots, early adopter programs |
| **Framing** | Same info, different angle | "14% turnover" vs "86% retention" |
| **Reciprocity** | Give first, get later | Free insights, free first team, free blog value |
| **Commitment/Consistency** | Small yes → big yes | Free trial → paid plan → enterprise |
| **Dunning-Kruger** | Overconfidence in unknown areas | CEOs think their culture is fine when data says otherwise |

### From "The Psychology of a Website" (Matthew Capala)
- Cognitive load: reduce choices, simplify CTAs
- Visual hierarchy: guide the eye to what matters
- Trust signals: logos, testimonials, certifications early on page
- Urgency without desperation
- Micro-copy matters: button text, form labels, error messages

### From "Six-Minute X-Ray" (Chase Hughes)
- People reveal priorities through language patterns
- Mirror language back to build rapport
- Behavioral baseline → deviation = signal
- In sales context: match prospect's communication style

### From "Exactly What to Say" (Phil M Jones)
- Magic phrases: "How would you feel if...", "What would happen if...", "Most people..."
- Presuppositions: embed the desired conclusion in the question
- "Just imagine..." — activates visual processing, emotionally anchoring

### From "Amplify Your Influence" (Rene Rodriguez)
- Storytelling > statistics (but stories WITH statistics = gold)
- Frame → message → close (the FMC method)
- Questions create ownership of ideas
- Vulnerability builds trust faster than perfection

## 9. EMAIL & OUTREACH FRAMEWORKS

### Cold Email Structure (from Cold Email Manifesto + AI Edge)
```
Subject: [Personalized, curiosity-driven, max 6 words]

Hi [Name],

[1 sentence showing you know them — specific, not generic]

[1-2 sentences about the problem you solve — framed as their pain]

[1 sentence with proof — number, case study, or social proof]

[1 sentence CTA — low commitment, specific]

[Signature]
```

### Email Sequence Framework (B2B SaaS)
1. **Day 1:** Value-first email (insight, stat, or question)
2. **Day 3:** Follow-up with case study
3. **Day 7:** Different angle (new pain point)
4. **Day 14:** Social proof (testimonial or logo wall)
5. **Day 21:** Breakup email ("Last thought on this...")

### Newsletter Structure (for Behavera blog emails)
- Subject: Hook/number + curiosity gap
- Opening: Story or shocking stat (2 sentences)
- Value: 3-5 key insights from the blog post
- CTA: "Read the full article" + secondary CTA "Try it free"
- PS: Bonus tip or upcoming content teaser

### Offer Email Structure
- Subject: Benefit + specificity
- Pain: "If you're dealing with [problem]..."
- Agitate: Consequences of inaction (loss aversion)
- Solution: What Behavera does (features → benefits)
- Proof: Case study or stat
- Offer: Specific, time-bound, risk-free
- CTA: One clear action

## QUICK REFERENCE: COPY FORMULAS

### Blog Post Hook Formulas
1. **Shocking stat:** "59 % zaměstnanců celosvětově je v režimu tichého odcházení."
2. **Story open:** "Mario Tulia řídí závod s 350 lidmi. Po prvním Echo Pulse řekl jednu větu..."
3. **Counterintuitive claim:** "Spokojenost zaměstnanců je past. Tady je proč."
4. **Direct question:** "Kolik vás letos stála tichá fluktuace?"
5. **Paradox:** "Čím víc firem měří engagement, tím hůř mu rozumí."

### CTA Formulas
- "Chcete vidět, jak to vypadá ve vaší firmě?" (curiosity)
- "Otestujte 1 tým zdarma" (low risk)
- "Spočítejte si to za svou firmu" (interactive)
- "Naplánujte si demo" (direct)
- "Stáhněte si šablonu" (content offer)

### Subject Line Formulas (emails)
- [Number] + [Benefit]: "3 čísla, která by měl znát každý CEO"
- [Question]: "Víte, kolik stojí odchod jednoho člověka?"
- [Curiosity gap]: "Tohle nám řekl šéf závodu s 350 lidmi"
- [Social proof]: "Proč 300+ českých firem přešlo na pulse"
- [Urgency]: "Vaši nejlepší lidé přemýšlí o odchodu. Data to potvrzují."
